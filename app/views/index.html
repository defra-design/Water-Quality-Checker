Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs

Check water conditions near you

Use the Water Intelligence Service to understand whether you can feel confident using rivers, lakes and coastal waters for swimming, paddling, fishing and other activities.

This service brings together trusted information from multiple organisations including the Environment Agency, Met Office, water companies, local authorities and citizen science projects.

What you can find out

  • whether conditions are good, need caution, or should be avoided today
  • recent sewage discharges, pollution incidents and algae warnings
  • rainfall, river levels, flow and water temperature
  • plain English advice for swimming, paddling, fishing and pets
  • the evidence behind the advice if you want to explore further

Who this is for

Anyone who uses or cares about open water — wild swimmers, paddleboarders, canoeists, anglers, dog walkers, parents, teachers, farmers, flood action groups and local communities.

This is an alpha discovery prototype. It does not replace services like Swimfo. It explores how Defra could provide a single, trusted view of water conditions.
